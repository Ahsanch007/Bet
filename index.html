Online Betting in India

Despite India’s historical obsession with betting on cricket, the wide availability of the internet and the heavy use of smartphones, it took time for online betting in India to be embraced compared to other countries.

Gambling in India is dictated by The Public Gambling Act of 1867, which prohibits most forms of gambling. But because it was written long, long before the invention of the internet, online betting wasn’t covered by it.

So essentially, online betting in India is a ‘grey area’ and the last few years has seen Indian betting companies and non-Indian ones welcoming Indian customers to their site.

It’s not ‘legal’ as such, but it’s also not illegal to do it so any Indian customer can have an online account.

Many have felt that the illegal Indian bookmaking industry has led to crime, corruption and money laundering and that online betting is a far better alternative.

It’s also true that any online betting site that accepts Indian customers has to pay tax to the Indian government, providing a valuable source of revenue.

Is Online Betting in India Legal?

What a question! The short answer is…it depends. Online betting laws technically say it is not illegal. The Public Gaming Act of 1867 doesn’t specifically mention� online� betting – for obvious reasons! So in that sense, it is a loophole that makes it not illegal.

However, only websites operated outside of India are legal. That’s why there are so many offshore legal sites for online betting in India.

If you’re living in Sikkim though, you are in luck. This is the only state in India that has completely legalised and regulated online betting. True heroes to the cause!

State Laws

The legality of online betting in India is determined on a state level. That means each state can choose to regulate betting as they please, or follow the Public Gambling Act of 1867. Many states have chosen to self-govern though. Here is what the legality of online betting is like in the top 5 states for visitors to Bet India:

  • Telangana – All forms of gambling are illegal here under the Telangana Gaming Act 2017.
  • Karnataka – Playing games of chance is illegal both online and offline under the Karnataka Police Act of 1963.
  • Maharashtra – Lottery and horse racing are completely legal here. Online betting is a grey area.
  • Uttar Pradesh – Betting house and other forms of physical gambling is illegal here. Online betting is perfectly fine!
  • Andhra Pradesh – All forms of gambling are illegal here under the Andhra Pradesh Gaming (Amendment) Act, 2020.

No Prosecutions

Still a bit worried about the online betting laws in India? You should know that no one has ever been prosecuted for online betting at legal betting sites from outside India.

Horse Racing and Lottery

Betting on horse races is completely legal throughout the country – since there is an element of skill involved when betting. Online lotteries are also legal in most states throughout India as well.

Rollover Requirements

So, these bonus funds sound pretty great, don’t they? A bit too great…What’s the catch? Well, the “catch” in this instance is what is called the rollover requirement. Now, if you’re new to all this online sports betting thing, you’ll probably never have heard this term before. It can be pretty tricky to get your head around.

Fear not though, you’re about to discover more on the topic – that way you’ll be prepared to use it to your advantage!

How does it work exactly?

Alright, let’s have a look at a simple example of this!

You just deposited ₹200 with a betting site that’s offering a 50% bonus and has a 10x rollover requirement.

You now have ₹300 to bet with – Nice!

However, that 10x rollover means that you can’t withdraw any of that juicy bonus money until you’ve placed ₹3,000 in bets.

((200+100) x10) = 3,000

Knowing this will give you a better understanding of what betting sites are worth signing up with and what promotions are worth your time and money.

Anything else to know?

First, be aware that there is a time limit on the rollover requirement. That means you need to be conscious of when you made claimed the bonus. Usually, the time frame will be anywhere from 30 days up to 90 days. So, you will have time to make your deposit at your chosen online betting site.

Secondly, and equally as important, is the odds requirement. This means that the rollover will not be applied to certain bets. For example, no betting sites will ever allow bets of 1.5 or lower to be used – it just wouldn’t make sense for them!

The best advice is to just read through the T&Cs in detail. Sure, it might be a bore, but it’s worth it.
